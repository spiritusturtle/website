Building Confidence in PE: Supporting Pupils with ADHD

Building confidence in PE often starts with predictable lesson routines. For pupils with ADHD, clear transitions, concise instructions, and visible success criteria can significantly reduce uncertainty and increase engagement.

Teacher confidence also grows when planning is framed around adaptation rather than perfection. Small adjustments such as shorter activity blocks, choice within tasks, and movement roles that distribute responsibility can improve participation for the whole class.

In practice, supportive PE environments are not built through one strategy. They emerge through consistent relational work: expectation setting, positive feedback, and reflection after each lesson on what helped pupils stay focused and successful.

The long-term goal is an inclusive PE culture where pupils with ADHD are not seen through deficits, but through their potential to thrive when pedagogy is responsive and intentional.
