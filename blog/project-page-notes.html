Three notes before beginning a project page

Before writing about a project, I try to answer three very small questions: what is the project, why does it matter, and what is the next concrete step?

That structure is useful because it keeps a project page practical. It prevents the page from becoming either a vague mission statement or a wall of implementation details.

The first version does not need every milestone. It only needs enough clarity for a future reader, collaborator, or curious visitor to understand the shape of the work.
